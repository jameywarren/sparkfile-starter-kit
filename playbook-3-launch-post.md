# Playbook 3: Draft the launch post you've been avoiding

**The job it does:** Gets a real first draft of your launch announcement out
of your head and onto the page.

**The prompt:**

```
We're launching: [describe what, in your own rough words]. Read VOICE.md for
how I write and PRODUCT.md for context.

Draft a launch post that:
1. Opens with what changed for the user, not with our internal milestone
2. Explains the problem this solves in one paragraph, plainly
3. Says what it doesn't do yet, if anything — under-claim rather than
   overclaim
4. Ends with one clear next step for the reader

Keep it under 300 words. I'll tell you what's wrong; don't try to guess and
hedge everything.
```

**What to watch for:**
- If the draft reads like a press release, that's VOICE.md needing more of
  your actual writing in it, not a prompt-wording problem.
- Resist puffing up the "what it doesn't do yet" section into a roadmap
  teaser — say the limitation plainly and move on. Under-claiming is the
  credibility move here, not filler.
